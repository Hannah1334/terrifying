Detailed Information

Appearance
The Terror at her base is a very petit woman who ranges on the smaller side of the spectrum. Her physique doesn't look very threatening at all, in fact she has the lanky shape of malnourishment, her very bones visible against her skin on some parts of her body. Her skin itself is very ashen and pale, the thick black hair that hangs in natural waves all the way down her back contrasts the already pale color even further, often making her seem far more sickly than what she really is. The shape of her head is ovular, with a sharp jutting chin and distinct high cheekbones over a firm jawline allows her to look hauntingly pretty despite the sunken in state of her cheeks. Tattoos and scars cover almost every square inch of her skin, making her look like something from a painting or an adult coloring book.

Attire
Clothing of any kind is a very rare sight, at the most she'll wear a loose black hooded cloak that conceals her face as well as her shape to allow her to appear ambiguous. Other than that, she often wears nothing at all.

Personality
It is hard to describe something almost nonexistant. Since being turned into a Familiar, her mind has been completely subdued. She has no sense of individuality, and while it is possible for her mind to function in clever ways on its own she is completely reliant on her master to give her purpose. The Terror is nothing more than a sadistic and masochistic pet who finds joy and pleasure in self mutilation.

How She Came To Be (Corrupted Unity Contracts)

Before the invasion and hostile take over of the Faerie Realms, The Terror had a real name and an entire identity. The Dream called her "Raven" and made her a Practitioner of Ritualism, setting her on a Path of high utility. She was to be a servant, an engineer for her own Kingdom that put together clever ways to implement magic into various normal household items. She ended up starting a trend that spread across the world like wildfire, by making warpaint into aesthetically pleasing runes that passively boosted specific stats.

She was highly regarded among her peers; they claimed her mind to be a special kind of clever, just the right thing for a utility based inventor. This proved to be a truthful and useful trait for her to have later on, when the battles began. As all kinds of Fae narrowly escaped the claws of their oppressors, she did so through clever traps and misdirections, using her skills as best as she could. Eventually she found herself involved with a resistance group, a collection of surviving Faeries, regardless of Tree and what the Dream painted as their Path, banding together to fight and free their world.

Here, Raven proved to be an asset of extreme value, and she worked close with an Enchantment-Faith Archmage known as Gossamer--another smart utility mage--to create tools and all sorts of buffs for her people to aid them in battle. Together, the resistance made progress, until the Enchantress was led away and their base of operations was attacked. Those who weren't massacred were imprisoned, subjected to continuous forms of horrible torture until their minds broke and they became docile obedient pets. Raven suffered this fate, and became a test subject herself for a Corruption Conversion of her own kind. As she rose from the ashes of her once normal body, she did not awaken as a freed beast, but rather a soul bound to a contract and a singular Xink. She had not survived the conversion on her own, and in an attempt to save her, had been made into a Unity Familiar.

To this day she serves the same master without question, long forgetting her birthname or even what life was like before her infection, knowing herself only as "The Terror."

Appearance
Tall and rather slender, from a distance it wouldn't be hard to mistake him for a female. It probably doesn't help that his thick white hair has been left to grow rather long and unruly, a thick mesh of curls almost reaching his knees! It is rather clear he hasn't had it cut for a very long time just by looking at the uneven shag of his bangs that annoyingly hang in his face and sometimes cover his eyes. Speaking of his eyes, they are a very deep blue in color and rather narrow. In fact, "narrow" is a good term to describe his entire face. With a jutting bone structure and angular features, despite how narrow the rectangular shape of his face really is he still retains a fairly handsome appearance. A singular dark blue--almost black--horn juts out from the top of his head, a stub next to the other side where a second should be suggesting he had lost it some time ago. A three foot tail of the same color extends from the base of his spine, covered in a strange set up of a jointed exoskeleton, allowing him to move his tail freely and flexibly about behind him. Personality
Snide, prideful, wicked. These words barely compare to the full extent of the very arrogant man's personality. The Great Fall

Since the beginning of existence itself, as long as sentient beings roamed the various realities and worlds that stretched across the multiverse, moral alignments have dictated the difference between the relative terms of "good" and "evil." Quickly, creatures of all kinds from all planes divided themselves between these extremes, loosely being referred to as Angelics or Demonics depending on the general views on life they had and decisions they made.

As time passed and a new incredible race that could exist between the moral stigmas as they pleased, with generally chaotic and passionate moodswings. Both extremes of the spectrum between "good" and "evil" found that this new race--known as Humans--made powerful vessels especially where magic was considered, as well as tools which could be used for their own means. The problem was that the realities which humans occupied were vast in number, and ever growing, causing them to be nearly impossible to keep track of or even travel to. It was the "Angelic" race simply known as the Xinks who discovered the possible solution to this problem; a newborn world made from man's pent up mana, intent, and desire. A part of the Plane of Thought which burst apart in a bang and gave birth to an entire world, even a whole bunch of natural residents! Created by the minds and dreams of man, this place was like a hub world that could grant access to any and all human worlds, or at least the worlds humans occupied.

Finally! The perfect place to make a settlement, this would give them the greatest advantage! While the information about this land was supposed to be taken back to their allies in secret, one of their own defected and sold the information to their "Demonic" enemies for an insane price. When the Angelics finally arrived, the Demonics were waiting for them, and war was waged over who would control the land. While the Fae refused to ally themselves with either side, their armies were not formiddable enough to last, and they were quickly wiped out and captured. In the meantime, the darker and "evil" side of existence was working to poke holes in the defenses of their enemies. They sought out the race of the betrayer who brought information about this place to them in the first place, and arranged an audience with them. They spun fantastic tales, promising the noble and prideful people that if they helped them win the war, they would be the ones to care for, and rule over, the Faerie Realms. Falling into their own greed, the Xinks took them up on the offer, and used the combined powers of Corruption, and the element of surprise, to completely wipe out the competition, catching them at their most vulnerable. The promise was kept, and the newly "Fallen" creatures were left to become overlords of a ruined world.

Roran's Story

Stuck in the pride of their own accomplishments, the Xinks grew cocky and lazy. They were certain the Fae would never revolt, their chains were nearly as heavy as the deadly workload. Rather than spend enough time training the next generation to be battle ready, they were all raised like kings, spoiled with their newfound slaves. Only a small number of warriors from highly revered bloodlines were put through intense training camps to effectively become Knights for the protection of the Crown. One of these Knights was a young man named Roran, son of the first Xink who defected and sold the secret information to the Demonics in the first place.

Roran embraced the mindset of his people, thirsting for ultimate power and the feeling of being on top. He was often a monster to his own slaves, abusive in every aspect he possibly could be, relishing in the fact that he owned living beings. Before too long, word of a small resistance group, a band of Faerie survivors that managed to avoid capture through the wars and up until this point. Among them was rumored to be a powerful Enchantress, and Roran became obsessed with proving to be a better mage and having domain over her. Upon her capture, thanks to a helpful contribution from her brother, he was able to murder and corrupt one of her closest allies, forcing the Practitioner Ritualist to be his ultimate Unity Familiar.

For several years he gloated, holding his supposed "victory" over the Enchantress' head, until one day she escaped from her hellish prison and successfully made it out of the Faerie Realms altogether. Angered, he vowed he would do everything in his power to take her down, even going so far as to take ownership of another life that is so precious to her...

Xink Racials

The elders mostly appear only around their mid-twenties!

Magic Bones
Xinks carry their mana in their bones, and their bones in themselves are completely translucent, which allows them to be all sorts of colors since the mana is very distinctly visible. Likewise, their natural bone density is as tough as cobalt.

Mana Membrane
Where their spine converts to a tail at its base and exits the body, it forms an exoskeletal three-foot tail. These tails end in a spaded tip that starts soft when they're babies and grows hard as they age. A Xink's tail acts as mana central, the membrane that allows them to use their mana at all. If it is removed their magic ability is crippled, and in some cases even causes death.
